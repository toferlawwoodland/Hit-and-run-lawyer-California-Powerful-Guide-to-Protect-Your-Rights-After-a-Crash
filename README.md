## Hit and run lawyer California: What Happens After a Driver Leaves the Scene?

A hit-and-run accident occurs when a driver involved in a collision leaves without stopping, identifying themselves, exchanging required information, or providing assistance when someone is injured.

California law requires drivers involved in accidents to follow certain responsibilities, including:

- Stopping at the accident scene
- Providing identification information
- Sharing insurance details when required
- Assisting injured individuals
- Reporting accidents when necessary

When a driver leaves the scene, victims often face additional difficulties. They must focus on recovering from their injuries while also trying to determine who caused the accident and how damages can be recovered.

### Why Hit-and-Run Accidents Are Difficult to Investigate

A typical accident investigation may involve both drivers exchanging information and insurance details. However, hit-and-run cases require a different approach because the responsible driver may not be immediately identified.

Important evidence may include:

- Security camera footage
- Traffic camera recordings
- Witness statements
- Vehicle debris
- Paint transfer
- Vehicle descriptions
- Police investigation records

Even small details may become valuable when attempting to identify the driver who left the scene.

### Common Reasons Drivers Leave After an Accident

Drivers may flee after a collision for different reasons, including:

- Fear of legal consequences
- Lack of insurance
- Driving under the influence
- Panic after the crash
- Attempting to avoid responsibility
- Outstanding legal issues

Regardless of the reason, leaving the scene can create additional hardship for injured victims.

### What Should You Do Immediately After a Hit-and-Run Accident?

The actions taken after a collision can affect the strength of a future claim.

### Contact Law Enforcement

Reporting the accident creates an official record.

A police report may include:

- Accident location
- Time of collision
- Vehicle description
- Witness information
- Evidence collected at the scene

Law enforcement may also investigate whether the responsible driver can be located.

### Seek Medical Treatment

Some injuries may not appear immediately after an accident.

Common delayed injuries include:

- Neck injuries
- Back injuries
- Soft tissue injuries
- Head injuries
- Concussions

Medical documentation can help establish the connection between the accident and the injuries.

### Preserve Accident Evidence

If possible, victims should collect:

- Photos of vehicle damage
- Pictures of the accident location
- Vehicle descriptions
- License plate information
- Witness contact information
- Nearby camera locations

Evidence can become critical when attempting to identify the responsible driver.

### Notify Your Insurance Company

Victims should report the accident to their insurance provider.

Depending on the policy, uninsured motorist coverage may help provide compensation when the responsible driver cannot be identified.
